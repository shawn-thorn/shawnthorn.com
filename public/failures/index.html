CV of Failures

Under Construction

Why this document? It’s important to recognize a failure when it happens, as a first step towards learning from the failure. That’s what this page is. In addition, I haven’t always done this – there have been times that I’ve been hesitant to call some failures a failure. I’m hoping this page will help me avoid sugar-coating failures in the future so that I can “fail better”.

This is definitely not an exhaustive list/discussion. In addition to sugar-coating failures, I’m relying on memory.

I’ve made this page public so that I can hopefully inspire others to take ownership over their failures too, but also to show everyone that I’m human, too. I got the idea from Mike Gelbart, but the idea seems to have originally stemmed from Melanie Stefan’s Nature article.

  • No published papers from my PhD
  • I never ended up submitting my first two R packages, copsupp and cnqr, to CRAN, because they are a mess and are incomplete.
    • I’m turning this around: I’m nearly finished a new-and-improved paper and analysis, and have new framework for new R packages (see distplyr and coperate).
  • Applied to several positions coming out of my PhD to no avail: including Boeing and Amazon.
  • Scholarships: I failed to get many, but two notable ones that I was not successful for was the Vanier and CGS-D scholarships (although I did get PGS-D).
  • I dropped an oenology and viticulture course in my undergrad because I couldn’t handle the workload of six courses, and I failed the midterm exam.
  • I struggled to get A’s in my biology courses in undergrad.
  • I signed up for a half marathon and didn’t run it because I failed to adequately prepare.
  • Several rejections for submissions to peer reviewed journals.
  • I’ve never won any formal individual fencing competitions in my ~6 years of fencing.
  • I once joined a kettlebell gym ($100+), and failed to go even once.
